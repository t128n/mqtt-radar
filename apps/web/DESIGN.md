## Look & feel

* **Radical minimalism**

  * Almost no visual noise.
  * The interface avoids decorative UI, gradients, cards, illustrations, and heavy branding.
  * It feels closer to a developer tool than a SaaS marketing site.

* **Docs-first, product-second aesthetic**

  * The landing/docs page looks like technical documentation, not a sales page.
  * The app view feels like an editor/workbench: direct access to structure and data, little ceremony.

* **Monochrome foundation**

  * Mostly black, white, gray, and subtle accent colors.
  * The palette is deliberately quiet, which makes the JSON content itself the visual focus.

* **High whitespace, low friction**

  * Large empty areas around content.
  * Nothing feels cramped, but it is also not “luxury whitespace”; it is functional breathing room.

* **Lightweight hierarchy**

  * Hierarchy comes from spacing, font weight, and alignment rather than boxes or strong color.
  * Headings are clear but not dramatic.
  * Navigation is present but visually secondary.

* **Soft technical precision**

  * Thin borders, subtle dividers, muted labels.
  * The UI feels precise and grid-aligned without looking enterprise-heavy.

* **Developer-native**

  * Monospace data areas, tree structures, compact controls, and file-like metaphors.
  * It feels familiar to people who use VS Code, GitHub, Vercel, Linear, or modern API/docs tools.

* **Calm interaction design**

  * Buttons and controls do not shout.
  * Active states are subtle.
  * The UI suggests: “use me for focused work,” not “look at me.”

* **Content over chrome**

  * The JSON/document content is the main object.
  * UI chrome is intentionally thin and disposable.
  * This is a good fit for tooling where the user’s data matters more than the product’s visual identity.

* **Modern open-source polish**

  * It has the look of a well-maintained OSS/devtool project:

    * sparse
    * neutral
    * fast-looking
    * pragmatic
    * slightly Vercel/GitHub-inspired

## In one sentence

The design is **minimal, monochrome, developer-focused, documentation-like, and intentionally quiet**, with just enough structure to feel polished without becoming visually heavy.
